19  /**
20    * Indicates that a failure has occurred that could impact the {@code
21    * Environment} as a whole. For failures that impact only the current
22    * operation and/or transaction, see {@link OperationFailureException}). For
23    * an overview of all exceptions thrown by JE, see {@link DatabaseException}.
24    *
25    * <p>Depending on the nature of the failure, this exception may indicate that
26    * {@link Environment#close} must be called. The application should catch
27    * {@code EnvironmentFailureException} and then call {@link
28    * Environment#isValid}. If {@code false} is returned, all {@code Environment}
29    * handles (instances) must be closed and re-opened in order to run recovery
30    * and continue operating. If {@code true} is returned, the {@code
31    * Environment} can continue operating without being closed and re-opened.
32    * Also note that {@link Environment#isValid} may be called at any time, not
33    * just during exception handling.</p>
34    *
35    * <p>The use of the {@link Environment#isValid} method allows JE to determine
36    * dynamically whether the failure requires recovery or not, and allows for
37    * this determination to change in future releases. Over time, internal
38    * improvements to error handling may allow more error conditions to be handled
39    * without invalidating the {@code Environment}.</p>
40    *
41    * <p>(Although this exception class extends {@link RunRecoveryException}, it
42    * does not always indicate that recovery is necessary, as described above.
43    * {@code RunRecoveryException} has been deprecated and {@code
44    * EnvironmentFailureException} should be used instead.)</p>
45    *
46    * <p>If an {@code EnvironmentFailureException} consistently occurs soon after
47    * opening the Environment, this may indicate a persistent problem. It may
48    * indicate a system problem or a persistent storage problem. In this case,
49    * human intervention is normally required and restoring from a backup may be
50    * necessary.</p>
51    *
52    * <p>Note that subclasses of {@code EnvironmentFailureException} indicate how
53    * to handle the exception in more specific ways.</p>
54    * <ul>
55    * <li>If {@code Thread.interrupt} is called for a thread performing JE
56    * operations, a {@link ThreadInterruptedException} is thrown. Since
57    * interrupting a thread is intentional, it does not indicate a persistent
58    * problem and human intervention is not normally required.
59    * </li>
60    * <li>If an {@code IOException} occurs while writing to the JE log, a
61    * {@link LogWriteException} is thrown. Although an {@code IOException} can
62    * occur for different reasons, it is a hint that the disk may be full and
63    * applications may wish to attempt recovery after making more disk space
64    * available.
65    * </li>
66    * <li>For replicated environments, see the subclasses of {@code
67    * EnvironmentFailureException} in the {@link com.sleepycat.je.rep} package for
68    * more information. Such exceptions may require special handling.
69    * </li>
70    * </ul>
71    *
72    * <p>If {@link Environment#close} is not called after an {@code
73    * EnvironmentFailureException} invalidates the {@code Environment}, all
74    * subsequent method calls for the {@code Environment} will throw the same
75    * exception. This provides more than one opportunity to catch and handle the
76    * specific exception subclass that caused the failure.</p>
77    */
